Understanding Emissions

Greenhouse Gases

  • Carbon Dioxide (CO₂): Produced mainly from burning fossil fuels and deforestation.
  • Methane (CH₄): Released from agriculture, landfills, and fossil fuel production.
  • Nitrous Oxide (N₂O): Emitted from fertilizers, waste, and some industrial activities.
  • Fluorinated gases: Industrial chemicals with very high warming potential.

Major Sources

  • Energy sector – electricity, heat, and transportation.
  • Industry – cement, steel, and chemical production.
  • Agriculture – livestock and fertilizer use.
  • Land-use change – deforestation and land degradation.
  • Waste – decomposition of organic matter in landfills.

Solutions

Transitioning to renewable energy, improving energy efficiency, adopting sustainable agriculture, reforestation, and technological innovations like carbon capture can significantly reduce emissions.
